---
title: GPX files
description: What GPX files are and where to get one for your cycling route.
---

A GPX file is a GPS track — a breadcrumb trail of coordinates that records a path. It's the standard format cycling apps use to store and share routes.

When you add a route to the site, you upload a GPX file and the map, distance, and elevation profile are generated automatically.

## Export from a cycling app

If you've already recorded a ride, you can export it as a GPX file from most cycling apps.

- **Strava** — [Exporting activities as GPX](https://support.strava.com/hc/en-us/articles/216918437-Exporting-your-Data-and-Bulk-Export#h_01GDP2JB35R4ECM0E6YAH316B9) (from strava.com, not the mobile app)
- **Garmin Connect** — [Exporting activities](https://support.garmin.com/en-CA/?faq=W1TvTPW8JZ6LfJSfK512Q8)
- **Komoot** — [Exporting routes and activities](https://support.komoot.com/hc/en-us/articles/10115477099674-Export-and-import-Routes-and-Activities#h_01KEFGA2F6YTVWHP0R5872KE5W)
- **Wahoo** — [Exporting GPX from Wahoo](https://gpxoverlay.com/tutorials/exporting-gpx-from-wahoo)

## Plan a new route

You don't need to ride a route before adding it. You can draw one on a map.

- **[gpx.studio](https://gpx.studio)** — Free browser-based route planner. Draw your route on the map and export as GPX. No account needed.
- **[RideWithGPS](https://ridewithgps.com)** — Route planner with cycling-specific features. You can paste a RideWithGPS link directly into the route creator instead of downloading a file.
- **[Komoot](https://www.komoot.com)** — Plan a tour and export the GPX.

## Import without a file

If your route is on RideWithGPS, you don't need to download anything. On the new route page, paste the RideWithGPS URL directly into the import field. The GPS track is fetched automatically.

Google Maps routes (created with [My Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/d/)) can also be imported by pasting the share link.
